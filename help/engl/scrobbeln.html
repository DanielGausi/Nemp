Scrobble with Nemp

To scrobble is a concept of last.fm - a social network with focus on music. Scrobling means to share the information what you are currently listening to with your friends. last.fm will then analyse your taste of music and recommends you other users with a similar one to find more music you may enjoy.

You can scrobble with Nemp.


Configure Nemp for scrobbling
At first Nemp needs access to your user profile on last.fm: Choose Settings -> last.fm click the button "Start", and follow the instructions.

Activate scrobbling
Within the settings dialog you can choose to scrobble just the current session (scrobble until you close Nemp), or to scrobble always. Scrobbling will start after click on the "Ok" button.
When Nemp is scrobbling your playlist, there will be an icon in the main window. You can stop scrobbling by a click on this icon.

What does Nemp do while scrobbling?
Scrobbling consists of two stages.
  • The "Now-Playing"-message. Every time a new track starts, Nemp will notify last.fm. On your LastFM profile will appear something like "<your name> currently listens to <current track>".
  • The "Submission"-Message. This notification is sent at the end of a track. Nemp will only "submit" the track, if at least 50% (or 4 minutes, whatever occurs first) of the track has been played (as it is recommended by last.fm). Short tracks (less than 30 seconds) are completely ignored. After a submit the track will appear in your history on last.fm.
Troubleshooting
Sometimes scrobbling doesn't work properly. Nemp will stop scrobbling in these cases. Possible error sources are
  • No internet connection
  • A firewall is blocking Nemp
  • The last.fm session key is invalid. You should restart the configuration to get a new session key.
  • Your account was blocked. You should contact last.fm for further assistance.
When you think, that you have fixed the source of error, you can click on "Scrobble again" in the settings dialog to try again.
